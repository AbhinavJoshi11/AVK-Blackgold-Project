How It Works

How Bitumen Emulsion Works

Understanding the emulsion process helps ensure correct selection and application on site.

1

Manufacturing

Hot bitumen and water are passed through a colloid mill with an emulsifier, producing stable bitumen droplets dispersed in water — IS 8887 / ASTM D2397 compliant.

2

Selection

Grade is chosen by application: RS for tack / surface dressing; SS1 for prime coat; SS2 for fog / slurry seal; SS2-Stab for stabilisation; MS for cold mix / patching.

3

Application

Applied via bitumen pressure distributor at ambient temperature — no heating needed — sprayed directly in a uniform film.

4

Breaking / Setting

Water evaporates and the emulsifier charge neutralises; droplets coalesce into a continuous film. RS breaks in ~15 min; SS grades break slower for better penetration.

5

Curing

After breaking, the bitumen film fully cures as remaining moisture is released. Curing depends on temperature, humidity and grade.

6

Trafficking

Once cured, the layer is ready for traffic or overlaying.
