Understanding RS1 grade emulsion for road construction

An introduction to RS1 bitumen emulsion and its role in road work.

By AVK Blackgold · 12 June 2026

RS1 (Rapid Setting grade 1) is a cationic bitumen emulsion — bitumen droplets suspended in water with an emulsifier. Being water-based, it can be sprayed at relatively low temperatures, which saves energy and improves site safety compared with hot bitumen.

As a rapid-setting grade, RS1 breaks quickly once it contacts aggregate: the water separates and the bitumen reverts to a continuous binder film in a short time. That makes it well suited to tack coats between pavement layers and to surface dressing, where a fast bond and early return to traffic matter.

Typical uses include tack coats ahead of bituminous overlays, surface dressing and chip seals on existing roads, and penetration work on granular bases. Correct application rate, clean surfaces and proper aggregate timing are key to a durable result.

At AVK Blackgold Infra, emulsions are produced using Prasol emulsifiers (such as Prasurf P-419 and P-591 for RS1), helping ensure consistent setting behaviour and storage stability across batches.
